PAST INCIDENT REPORT
Incident ID: INC-2024-0312
System: BEACON Benefits Portal
FISMA Category: CAT 2 — Denial of Service
Severity: P1
Date: March 12, 2024
Duration: 1 hour 47 minutes
Citizens Affected: ~31,000

SUMMARY
BEACON citizen portal experienced complete unavailability during Tuesday morning
filing window due to database connection pool exhaustion. Root cause was a
configuration drift: the prod environment had maximumPoolSize=5 while staging
had maximumPoolSize=20 after an unreviewed config change three weeks prior.

ROOT CAUSE
Config drift between environments. A developer updated staging application.properties
to test higher pool sizes but the change was not included in the prod deployment.
When the monthly benefit processing job ran alongside citizen filing traffic,
the pool of 5 connections was exhausted within 4 minutes.

TIMELINE
08:42 AM — Benefit processing job starts (scheduled)
08:46 AM — First HikariPool warning in Splunk
08:48 AM — First citizen-facing 503 errors
08:51 AM — CloudWatch alarm triggers, PagerDuty pages on-call
09:03 AM — Root cause identified via Splunk + GitHub comparison
09:15 AM — Emergency CAB convened (phone), pool increase approved
09:28 AM — Hotfix deployed
09:35 AM — Error rate returns to normal
10:35 AM — Incident closed

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN
1. [COMPLETED] Increase maximumPoolSize to 20 in all environments
2. [COMPLETED] Add config drift detection to deployment pipeline
3. [COMPLETED] Add HikariPool utilization alert at 75%
4. [COMPLETED] Separate citizen filing traffic from batch job traffic (load balancer rules)
5. [IN PROGRESS] Capacity planning review for peak windows

US-CERT REPORTING
Reported to US-CERT within 2-hour window: YES
FISMA CAT 2 report filed at 10:47 AM (59 minutes after detection)

LESSONS LEARNED
- Config drift between environments is a silent P1 risk
- Pool exhaustion warning should alert at 75%, not 100%
- Batch jobs should run in off-peak windows or separate connection pools
- On-call engineers need direct RDS console access to diagnose faster
