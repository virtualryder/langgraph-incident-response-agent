NIST SP 800-61 REV 2 — INCIDENT HANDLING GUIDANCE
Category 2: Denial of Service
Source: NIST Special Publication 800-61 Revision 2, Section 3.2

MANDATORY REPORTING TIMELINE
Federal agencies must report CAT 2 incidents to US-CERT within 2 hours of detection.
State agencies receiving federal funding must follow state ISSO reporting chain
within 2 hours, which triggers federal notification if cross-agency impact exists.

REQUIRED REPORTING FIELDS (US-CERT Federal Incident Notification)
1. Incident category and functional impact
2. Information impact (PII/CUI exposure determination)
3. Recoverability (recoverable, recoverable with supplemental resources, not recoverable)
4. Number of records/systems affected
5. Attack vector (if known)
6. Current status
7. Mitigating factors
8. Point of contact for follow-up

CONTAINMENT STRATEGIES FOR CAT 2 DoS
Immediate actions:
- Identify and isolate affected components
- Apply rate limiting or traffic shaping if external attack
- Isolate batch/background processes from citizen-facing traffic
- Scale out if cloud infrastructure allows

Eradication:
- Fix root cause before restoring full service
- Do not restore service to a broken state — validate health checks

Recovery:
- Restore service incrementally, monitoring error rates
- Validate with synthetic transaction before public announcement
- Document all changes made during incident for post-mortem

POST-INCIDENT ACTIVITIES (NIST 800-61 Section 3.4)
Required within 30 days:
- Lessons learned meeting with all responders
- Post-incident report including timeline, root cause, and corrective actions
- Update incident response plan if gaps identified
- Brief CISO and system owner

EVIDENCE PRESERVATION
All logs, configurations, and tool outputs must be preserved for:
- 3 years minimum (standard federal records retention)
- Duration of any related audit or investigation
- IG review if citizen impact > 10,000 records or > $100,000 estimated damage

PRIVACY ACT CONSIDERATIONS (CAT 2 Specific)
CAT 2 incidents are availability incidents — typically do NOT trigger Privacy Act
notification unless error logs, debug output, or application errors expose PII.
Review application error logs carefully for session tokens, SSNs, or benefit data
before closing incident as non-PII.
